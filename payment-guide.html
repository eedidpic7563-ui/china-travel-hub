How to Pay in China in 2026

Updated: May 2026 · ✓ Verified

China is essentially a cashless society. Alipay and WeChat Pay dominate everything from street food to luxury hotels. This guide covers how to set up mobile payments, what alternatives exist, and how to avoid common pitfalls.

⚠️ Set up BEFORE arriving. Alipay and WeChat Pay accounts require a Chinese phone number and verification. Setting this up before your trip saves major headaches.

1. Payment Options at a Glance

Method Ease of Use Requirements Fees
Alipay ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Chinese phone + ID verification 0% (linked card)
WeChat Pay ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Chinese phone + ID verification 0% (linked card)
Cash (RMB) ⭐⭐ None None
Foreign Cards ⭐⭐ Visa/MasterCard accepted 2-3% foreign transaction

2. Setting Up Alipay (Recommended)

Alipay is the most widely accepted mobile payment in China. Even small vendors accept it.

1

Download Alipay App

  • Download Alipay from App Store / Play Store
  • Sign up with your email or phone number
  • Set a strong password and enable biometric login
2

Verify Your Account

You'll need to complete identity verification. For foreigners:

  • Tap "Authenticate" or "Verify ID"
  • Select passport as ID type
  • Take a photo of your passport + selfie
  • Verification typically takes 1-3 days
3

Add a Foreign Card

  • Go to "Bank Cards" → "Add Card"
  • Most major foreign cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex) work
  • Some banks require verification via SMS or app
  • After linking, you get a virtual "China UnionPay" card
💡 Pro tip: Add money to your Alipay balance before traveling. Some merchants prefer balance over linked cards.
4

Get a Chinese Phone Number (Required)

This is the trickiest part for tourists. Options:

  • Buy a SIM at the airport: China Mobile, China Unicom, Telecom. Cost: ¥50-100 ($7-14)
  • Use your home carrier's roaming: Some work with Alipay verification
  • Virtual phone services: Some travelers use services like PingMe or TextNow (unreliable)

3. Setting Up WeChat Pay

WeChat Pay is essential because WeChat itself is how people communicate in China.

1

Download WeChat

  • Download WeChat (Weixin) from app store
  • Sign up with your phone number
  • Verify with SMS code
2

Enable WeChat Pay

  • Go to Me → Wallet → Cards
  • Tap "Add Card" and follow verification steps
  • Same passport verification process as Alipay
  • Link your foreign credit/debit card
💡 Use WeChat for: Communicating with Chinese contacts, paying at merchants who prefer WeChat, sending red envelopes (hongbao) to friends.

4. Using Cash

Cash still works everywhere but is increasingly inconvenient. Here's how to manage it:

  • Exchange before arrival: Get some RMB from your bank or currency exchange
  • ATM withdrawals: Most ATMs accept foreign cards (2-3% fee). Look for China UnionPay or international ATMs
  • Bank counter: Large banks (ICBC, Bank of China) can exchange currency with passport
⚠️ Carry small bills. Many small vendors and taxis don't have change for large bills (¥100).

5. Using Foreign Cards Directly

Some places accept Visa/MasterCard directly. Expect hassles.

  • Hotels: Most 4-5 star hotels accept foreign cards
  • Large restaurants: Upscale restaurants may accept cards
  • Shopping malls: International brands usually accept foreign cards
  • Street food / small shops: Almost never accept foreign cards
💡 Don't rely on it: Cards work at maybe 10% of places. Mobile payment (Alipay/WeChat) is practically required.

6. Common Payment Scams

⚠️ Fake payment screens. Some taxis show fake Alipay/WeChat QR codes. Always verify the merchant's official code.
⚠️ "Lucky Money" scams. Don't scan random QR codes on street. Some are malicious or connected to scams.
⚠️ Card skimmers. Use ATMs inside banks, not street ATMs. Cover your PIN.

7. Pre-Trip Checklist

  1. ✅ Download Alipay and WeChat apps
  2. ✅ Research getting a Chinese SIM card at airport
  3. ✅ Verify your ID on Alipay (start 1 week early)
  4. ✅ Link a credit card to Alipay
  5. ✅ Carry $50-100 USD cash as backup
