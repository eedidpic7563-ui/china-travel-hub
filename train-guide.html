How to Travel by Train in China in 2026

Updated: May 2026 · ✓ Verified

China has the world's largest high-speed rail network. Trains are fast, comfortable, affordable, and the easiest way to travel between major cities. This guide covers everything you need to know about tickets, routes, and tips.

🚄 High-speed trains (高铁): Reach 300+ km/h. Most comfortable option. Book 2-3 weeks ahead for holidays.

1. How to Book Train Tickets

1

Official Apps (Recommended)

  • 12306: The official China Railway app. Best for finding real-time availability and paying with foreign cards
  • Trip.com (Ctrip): Good English interface, slight booking fee
  • Trainpal: Sometimes has discounts, clear seat selection
2

Booking with Foreign Passports

  • All three apps above support passport booking
  • You'll need to enter your passport number exactly as it appears
  • Some apps require phone number verification — use your hotel's number or a Chinese SIM
  • Foreign cards (Visa, MasterCard) are accepted on 12306 and Trip.com
3

What to Expect After Booking

  • You'll receive an e-ticket with a QR code
  • Pick up the physical ticket at the station using your passport and the pickup code
  • Look for the "Foreign Passenger" ( foreign passenger) counter or use the self-service machines (新版自助机)
  • You need the physical ticket to go through the gate — QR codes alone don't work at most stations
⚠️ Pick up tickets BEFORE entering the platform. Have your passport ready. The ticket pickup machines accept foreign passports. Allow 30 minutes before departure.

3. Understanding Train Types

  • G (高铁): High-speed, 300+ km/h. Most common between major cities
  • D (动车): High-speed, 200-250 km/h. Slightly slower, cheaper than G
  • C (城际): Inter-city trains. Short routes like Shanghai-Hangzhou
  • K/T/Z: Regular trains. Slower (100-150 km/h) but cheaper. Good for overnight
Seat Classes: 二等座 (2nd class / Type-C), 一等座 (1st class / Type-A), 商务座 (Business class / Type-S). Business class is ¥2-3x first class but has massive recline.

4. Station Tips

1

Getting to the Station

  • Major stations are HUGE. Allow extra time to find your platform
  • Metro is usually the fastest way — many cities have lines connecting to train stations
  • Didi (Chinese Uber) works great for direct station transfers
  • Taxis at train stations have designated waiting areas — follow the signs
2

What to Bring

  • Passport — You'll need it for ticket pickup and ID check at the gate
  • Physical ticket — Keep it handy for safety checks and the platform gate
  • Water and snacks — Onboard food is expensive and limited
3

Boarding Process

  • Arrive 30-40 minutes before departure (20 minutes for short routes)
  • Go through security check (like airport, but no liquid restrictions)
  • Show ticket + passport at the platform gate
  • Find your car number (车厢) printed on your ticket
  • Board when the platform guard calls your car

5. Train Etiquette

  • No eating in carriages: Some trains allow it, but strong-smelling food is frowned upon
  • Keep voice down: Phone calls should be brief, loud conversations are rude
  • Luggage goes above or at your feet — don't block the aisle
  • Boarding is orderly: Line up, wait for others to exit first
  • No smoking: Smoking is banned on all high-speed trains (even between carriages)
⚠️ Violently enforced: Smoking on high-speed trains can result in fines of 500-2000 RMB and a permanent travel ban. Don't do it.

6. Pre-Trip Checklist

  1. ✅ Download 12306 or Trip.com app
  2. ✅ Add your passport to the app before booking
  3. ✅ Book tickets 2+ weeks ahead for popular routes (Beijing-Shanghai, holiday travel)
  4. ✅ Carry your passport to the station
  5. ✅ Allow 30-40 minutes for ticket pickup and security
