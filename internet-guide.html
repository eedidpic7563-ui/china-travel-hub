How to Stay Online in China in 2026

Updated: May 2026 · ✓ Verified — Tested May 2026

Google, YouTube, Facebook, WhatsApp, Instagram, GPT, Wikipedia — all blocked in China. The Great Firewall is real, and it's pervasive. Here's exactly how to stay connected and access the open internet during your trip.

⚠️ Do this BEFORE your trip. Paid VPNs often require account setup before arriving. Free options are unreliable or unsafe. Do this at home.

1. Get an eSIM for China

An eSIM is a digital SIM — no physical card needed. You buy it online, scan a QR code, and you're connected. Best for tourists.

2

Buy and Install

  • Buy on the provider's website — pay with your regular credit card
  • You'll get a QR code via email
  • iPhone: Settings → Cellular → Add Cellular Plan → Scan QR
  • Android: Settings → Network & Internet → Mobile Network → Add Carrier → Scan QR
  • Turn on the eSIM line and set it as default for data
⚠️ Enable BEFORE landing. Some eSIMs only activate when you scan them in coverage. Do this at home or in the airport before boarding your China flight.

2. Set Up a VPN

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your traffic and routes it through a server outside China, bypassing the Great Firewall. Essential for Google, YouTube, WhatsApp, and more.

Install & Configure

  • Sign up before you leave home. Most VPNs require account creation.
  • Download the app for all your devices (phone, laptop, tablet)
  • Test it now — verify you can connect to a server outside China before you land
  • In China, launch the VPN and connect to a server (Hong Kong, Japan, or Singapore usually fastest)

3. Alternative: Roaming + International Plans

Don't want eSIM? Here's what else works:

  • Your home carrier's international roaming: Check if your carrier offers China roaming. Usually expensive ($10-15/day for data) but works out of the box.
  • Local Chinese SIM card: You can buy a SIM at the airport or convenience store (you'll need your passport). Expect to pay ¥50-100 for the SIM + ¥30-50 for data packages. This WORKS great but requires some Mandarin or a helpful staff member.
⚠️ DON'T rely on free hotel WiFi for sensitive stuff. Hotel WiFi often works for basic browsing but: (1) it's slow, (2) many sites are blocked anyway, (3) it's not secure for passwords or banking.

4. Your Pre-Trip Checklist

  1. ✅ Buy and install an eSIM (Airalo recommended)
  2. ✅ Set up a VPN before departure
  3. ✅ Install WhatsApp, Line, or WeChat (for communication in China)
  4. ✅ Download offline maps (Google Maps offline maps or Maps.me)
  5. ✅ Screenshot important info: eSIM QR code, VPN login, embassy contact
